§ II · The Cost of a Missed Signal

What gets overlooked when a human is reading the data alone.

Data lives in silos, alerts fire out of context, and a small miss compounds fast. These are real patterns from one sample engagement — each was already costing the operator money for days or weeks before WellRX surfaced it. Dollar figures are grounded in observed MCF deltas at $3.50/MCF, not projections.

Gathering event masked as separate well declines

A cluster of meters on one CTB network showed simultaneous 150–400% static-pressure creep over 5–7 days. To a field team reading well-by-well: several wells each losing a little, probably decline. To WellRX's virtual AI trio: one midstream bottleneck, one fix. Chemistry and lift money spent on individual wells was actively wasted while the real upstream problem sat invisible.

Recoverable · one fix~$17,000/mo

Liquid loading misread as reservoir decline

DP rising while MCF flattens is a textbook load-up — a $2/day soap-stick campaign fixes it. Misread as a dying well: the operator schedules a $10–40k plunger install, a velocity-string swap, or begins decom planning. WellRX flags the divergence within 24 hours of onset, before anyone reaches for the AFE pad.

Capex avoided per well$10k–$40k

Live-but-dark RTU hiding real production

Several wells went quiet mid-window while still producing 40–130 MCF/D. A busy field team reads "offline = maybe decommissioned" and moves on. WellRX separates dark-but-producing from truly offline on day one. A handful of $300 truck rolls recover full revenue visibility and ~$33k/mo of gas that was invisible to the dashboard.

Exposure · $/mo invisible~$33,000

Paraffin diagnosed as plunger problem

Temp floor drops below 52°F with intermittent DP pulses. To a human scanning the dashboard: "plunger's not cycling right — call the AL vendor." To a virtual AI chemist reading the thermal signature: paraffin dropout. A $400/mo continuous inhibitor fixes it; a $10k plunger swap doesn't. Catching the right cause on day one avoids a wasted workover.

Per-well capex saved$10k+
