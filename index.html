§ I · What WellRX Is

Every well is telling you what's wrong. WellRX hears every signal — live or historical — diagnoses, and writes the Rx.

Most gas-well operators look at their production through a spreadsheet — SCADA in one tab, FSR logs in another, gauge sheets on paper, delivery tickets in email, alarms in a dashboard nobody refreshes. Thresholds fire, the field gets busy, and production slips a few percent a month until someone loses a well. WellRX wires into whatever data you've already got — SCADA (zdSCADA live today, generic adapters for the rest), historian exports, FSR records, gauge sheets, photos from the field — runs every well through a petroleum-engineering diagnostic every morning, and puts a specific, ROI-scored action on the right person's desk before coffee. Your pumper gets a field route brief, not a dashboard. Your production specialist gets a daily Rx, not a raw data dump. Your foreman gets a sign-off queue. Your exec team gets a portfolio diagnostic that reads like a memo from a VP of Production.

§ IV · Integration & Delivery

Where WellRX runs and how your team receives it.

SCADA systems
Live zdSCADA (OAuth2, daily + lp + sv + g + ev + config channels)   Adapter Any SCADA with a REST / SFTP / JDBC feed — add a driver, everything downstream is unchanged.
Ingestion cadence
Full daily pull at 05:45 local; hourly spot-value snapshot for live alarms; weekly config/gas-composition refresh.
Diagnosis engine
Petroleum-engineering rules (Coleman critical-velocity loading, decline-curve detection, FT / BF / temp anomaly scoring, CUSUM for gathering events). Human-readable output.
Delivery channels
Live Email (HTML body + xlsx / PDF attachments per recipient role)   Roadmap Web portal (operator dashboard, case-file archive, team permissioning)
Recipient profiles
Every recipient has a role (vendor, pumper, engineer, executive), a well scope, a delivery channel, and a custom report template. One SCADA feed, N tailored outputs — each with its own diagnosis and Rx per role.
Data security
Single-tenant credentials held in the operator's keychain; auth worker runs on-prem or in your cloud. No raw telemetry leaves the boundary you control.
§ V · The Fine Print

Deployment, pricing, and who's behind the Rx.

Deployment
~2 weeks from data handshake to first Rx in inbox. Our launch engagement went live in under 10 days — auth worker stood up, first daily pull, first prescription email on the production specialist's phone. We don't touch your SCADA config; we read what you already have.
Who can buy
Operator-wide — the company buys the feed once, and every role gets their own Rx (pumpers, production specialists, foremen, engineering, executive). One integration, N tailored outputs, team pricing.   Individual role-based — a single production engineer, chemistry vendor rep, foreman, or contractor pumper can also subscribe for just their own daily Rx against the operator's feed (with operator consent). A leg up on your peers without waiting on the company to roll it out.
Pricing model
Portfolio-sized for operator-wide engagements — low four-figures monthly, priced to pay for itself in the first month on observed MCF recovery. Individual role subscriptions are seat-based and start well under that. Vendor-facing and on-demand diagnostics price separately. Ask Chris — we'll run the ROI math against your own data before anyone signs anything.
Security posture
Operator-held credentials · single-tenant deployment · no raw telemetry leaves the boundary you control · email delivery uses a sender you authorize. SOC 2 Type II on the roadmap alongside the web portal.
